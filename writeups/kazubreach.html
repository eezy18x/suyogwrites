🚨 Massive Breach Hits Nepal's Ministry of Education

🗓️ Published: July 2025

✍️ Author: Suyog Jung Karki

NepalCyberAttack MoESTBreach KAZU InfoSec

🧠 What Happened?

A threat actor named KAZU claims responsibility for breaching Nepal’s Ministry of Education, Science, and Technology (MoEST)—a platform used by students applying abroad for NOC documentation.

The actor announced via Telegram and underground channels that 1.4TB of sensitive data was stolen and listed for sale on July 14, 2025, priced from $2,000 to $10,000 depending on volume.

🗂️ Leaked Data Includes:

  • 📇 Personal identification
  • 🎓 Education records
  • 🛂 Passport and citizenship scans
  • 📄 NOC-related documents
  • 🗨️ Internal ministry communications

A 100GB sample was released publicly to counter denial by MoEST and validate the breach.

📎 Telegram Sample: https://t.me/+0ce6J1cCyT4xMzA0

🎯 Ministry's Response

After media outlets like TechPana picked up the story, MoEST responded stating:

"He cannot access our system under any circumstances."
"Our ministry does not have 1.4TB of data."
"He is doing this just to gain attention."

However, after the 100GB sample dropped, cybersecurity experts called out the denial as reckless and misleading.

💰 Ransom Demand

KAZU has demanded $20,000 in Monero (XMR) within 10 days to stop further leaks.

If unpaid, the attacker promises to release the remaining 1.3TB in phases.

⏳ No negotiations. No delays.

📣 Why It Matters

This isn’t just a tech incident—it’s a national digital security crisis.

  • 👨‍🎓 Students may face exposure of personal education records
  • 🇳🇵 Citizens risk identity theft or service denial
  • 🔐 Confidence in public infrastructure has taken a hit

🚨 Recommended Actions

  • 🔍 Full security audit of MoEST infrastructure
  • 🕵️ Independent investigation by Nepal CERT or third-party forensic teams
  • 📢 Public statement from MoEST outlining action and transparency

For now, the breach is active and developing—and the world is watching. 🕶️
